Examples of the capabilities of BayesX

In the following we demonstrate the capabilities of BayesX with two complex examples:

Study on unemployment durations

In this example we present an analysis of official unemployment data from the German Federal Employment office. The primary focus of the study is to estimate the probability that an unemployed person gets a new job in the presence of covariates (e.g. age, calendar time, etc.). Typical questions arising in studies on duration of unemployment are: What is the baseline effect (duration dependence)? How can trend and seasonal effects of calendar time be incorporated in a flexible manner? Is there an age effect? Are there regional differences for the probability of leaving unemployment and getting a new job?

Munich rents for flats

According to the German rental law, owners of apartments or flats can base an increase in the amount that they charge for rent on "average rents" for flats comparable in type, size, equipment, quality and location in a community. To provide information about these "average rents", most larger cities publish "rental guides", which can be based on regression analyses with rent as the dependent variable. We present here a Bayesian analysis of a data set that has been collected in 1998 by Infratest Sozialforschung for the Munich rental guide 1999. The data set contains information on more than 3000 flats in Munich.
